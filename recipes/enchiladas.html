Enchiladas

Description

Morelian enchiladas, also known as placeras, are the typical dish of Michoac√°n gastronomy, and this time we bring you the recipe. This delicious delicacy stands out from other styles due to the unique flavor of the sauce, and the side dishes that accompany it, so take note!

Ingredients

  • 6 guajillo chilies deveined and cooked (reserve the water where you cooked them)
  • 1 teaspoon of oregano
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • 4 peppercorns to grind at the moment
  • 2.2 lbs of carrots cut into squares
  • 2.2 lbs of potatoes cut into squares
  • 1 head of lettuce
  • .6 lbs of dry cotija cheese
  • 10 oz of onion slices
  • Half an onion
  • Fried chicken
  • Chilli peppers
  • Lard or oil
  • 2 lbs of tortillas
  • 10 oz of chopped onion
  • 7 oz of grated cotija cheese

Steps

  1. Cook the carrots and potatoes.
  2. Once cooked, fry them in a pan with preheated oil, and reserve
  3. In a blender place the chiles with the garlic, oregano, the water where you cooked them and half an onion, and blend until you achieve the consistency of a sauce.
  4. Place the mixture in a deep container, where you are going to soak each tortilla, and fry in a preheated pan with the lard or oil.
  5. Fill with the cheese and onion and if you wish, the shredded chicken and fold in three.
  6. On a plate place the enchiladas on a bed of lettuce, and on top put a tablespoon of potatoes and carrots, the onion slices, the cheese and you can add cream. Bon Appetite!
